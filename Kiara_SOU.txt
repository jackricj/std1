Simple Columnar Epithelium

Goblet cells are specialized secretory cells found within the simple columnar epithelium. They secrete mucus, which acts as a protective and lubricating layer. An example of this can be seen in the lining of the digestive tract, particularly in the small intestine, where the simple columnar epithelium with goblet cells secretes mucus to aid in the movement of food and protect the lining from digestive enzymes.
