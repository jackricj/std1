Connective tissues perform several essential functions in the human body, including:

Structural Support: Due to their ability to form the skeleton structure of tissues, organs, and the whole body, they give form and support to their bodies.

Binding and Connecting: They bind the various types of tissue together, thus holding other organs and tissue structures in place. For example, Ligaments connect the bones to each other. On the other hand, tendons connect the muscles to bones.

Protection and Defence: Some types, such as bone, do indeed provide protective covering to internal organs. Additionally, connective tissue contains specialized cells that are very important in the body's immune response system.

Know that insulation and storage come from adipose tissue, one of the types of connective tissue, which basically accommodates fat, acting to insulate the body to maintain temperature and storing energy when required.

Transport: Blood is a type of connective tissue and it performs an important function in the transportation of oxygen, nutrition, waste products, hormones etc., within the body. Tissue Repair: Fibrous connective tissue plays a key role in wound healing by forming scar tissue.
