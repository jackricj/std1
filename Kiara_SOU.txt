Its structure makes transitional epithelium suitable to serve its main purpose: providing both distensibility and protection to organs that change a great deal in volume. For example, the lining of the urinary bladder with transitional epithelium allows it to expand when full of urine and then recoils back when the urine is expelled. The transitional epithelium has a unique property of change of shape and stretching without tearing or disrupting the integrity of the epithelial barrier, allowing changes in organ volume while still providing protection in an impermeable way against urine and its toxic contents.
