Essentially, the transitional epithelium differs in structure from every other kind of stratified squamous in that it can do this stretch and then recoil without damage. Transitional epithelium feels the outside, it is made of many layers of tissue, and its cells stretch into various shapes reversible when an organ such as the urinary bladder is filled with urine. The cells of the transitional epithelium are somewhat rounded or dome-shaped when the organ is empty, and they become flattened and stretched out once the organ is distended.
